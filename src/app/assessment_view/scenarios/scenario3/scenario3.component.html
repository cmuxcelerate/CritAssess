Scenario 3 of 7

Your company is building a learning management system that’s specially tailored to K12 project-based learning. After extensive user research, your UX department provided you with a list of features that teachers and students want to include in the product.

  • Upload Project Proposal
  • Define Learning Goals
  • Set Weekly Goals
  • Upload Daily Progress
  • Add Collaborators
  • Make Audio Comments
  • View Achievement Visualization
  • Upload Self-reflection
  • Receive a Detailed Achievement Report

Here are some quotes from students and teachers about what they expect to have in the product that your UX team reported to you:

Quote 1 (Student) : “I think the adding weekly goals is a really useful function as it helps me know what I am supposed to achieve by the end of the week.”

Quote 2 (Teacher) :“I think the adding weekly goals is pretty important because you could easily check students’ progress. Making audio comments might be a great feature as well as it could save me some time in grading students’ work.”

Quote 3 (e-learning director) : Our research team has found that if students upload self-reflection online, it would help improve their performance.”

There are already some equivalent products on the market, and your marketing department has provided you with a competitive matrix with the features that each product has. At current stage, your company wants to build a MVP (minimal viable product) that covers basic features but could also differentiate itself in this niche market.
